# Dispatches from the Record: Interlude

**The Public Dollar Cannot Enter a Black Box**  
**A Model Appropriations Rider for a Private Presidential Settlement Fund**

## What is this?

This is a public civic artifact and model appropriations rider.

It gives members of Congress, staff, journalists, watchdogs, and citizens usable language for blocking public money from being obligated, certified, disbursed, or administered through a private presidential settlement fund unless Congress enacts a specific appropriation identifying the settlement, purpose, and maximum amount.

## Why should I care?

The public dollar must remain traceable.

When public money leaves the Treasury, the public should be able to identify what Congress authorized, what purpose the money serves, how much exposure Congress permitted, who may receive the money, why they may receive it, and what institution can audit the result.

A permanent appropriation is not a blank check for an executive-controlled settlement channel.

## What is the claim?

The core claim is that appropriations traceability is a constitutional maintenance condition.

A public dollar should carry a legible chain from congressional authorization, to lawful purpose, to fixed exposure, to eligible claimant, to accountable disbursement. When that chain breaks, the public fisc enters a black box.

## What is this not?

This is not legal advice.

It is not a full constitutional brief, litigation memo, or exhaustive analysis of the Judgment Fund. It is a public-use model rider and civic explanation designed for immediate use, review, adaptation, and public circulation.

## How was this done?

The artifact translates a live public-money problem into a model appropriations limitation. It uses the language of appropriations traceability, public authorization, capped exposure, record preservation, and oversight access.

It also frames the issue through Constitutional Physiology and Structural Flow: when authority, conflict, claimant, injury, maximum exposure, payment, and accountability separate from one another, the public fisc is no longer maintained as a public organ answerable to the whole constitutional body.
